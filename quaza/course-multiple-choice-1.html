LEXIS AND STRUCTURE

SECTION II

From the list of words lettered A to D choose the one that best completes each of the following sentences.

9. Janet gave up her late father's house on her own ....
A. intention
B. volition
C. decision
D. initiative

10. The researchers were expected to collect and ... date on the subject.
A. collate
B. correct
C. classify
D. compile

11. All of the illegal immigrants were ....
A. exiled
B. repelled
C. repatriated
D. expunged

12. The students went on a ... through the town destroying everything in their way.
A. strike
B. campaign
C. protest
D. rampage

13. The holy day was duly observed by the ... followers.
A. concerned
B. devout
C. extreme
D. rigid

14. Ama has always been a/an ... student.
A. respective
B. ambient
C. respectful
D. amicable

15. George's credentials show that he is ... to manage the firm.
A. skilful
B. apt
C. equal
D. qualified

16. The crime was prevented by the ... of the police.
A. security
B. guard
C. protection
D. vigilance

17. The young footballer attracts many fans; he will soon become a ....
A. leader
B. star
C. champion
D. winner

18. Many of the pupils played truant because of ... punishment.
A. plenty
B. excessive
C. numerous
D. great

19. Kwaku gave us the impression that he had stopped drinking but has sinced ....
A. begun
B. resumed
C. continued
D. renewed

20. Kojo is not very intelligent but has ... in tailoring.
A. skills
B. talents
C. capabilities
D. potentials

SECTION III

After each of the following sentences, a list of possible interpretations of all or part of the sentence is given. Choose the interpretation you consider most appropriate for the sentence.

21. When the two enemies met, they squared up to each other. This means that they
A. shook hands.
B. growled at each other.
C. looked at each other.
D. got ready to fight.

22. Being the oldest, Jacob was given the lion's share of the booty. This means that he was
A. given what he did not deserve.
B. given the biggest portion.
C. treated like a king.
D. treated like one to be feared.

23. Lord Kenya's album was a runaway success. This means that the album
A. was hugely successful.
B. was his latest.
C. gave him an award.
D. made him proud.

24. The students took issue with the Vice-Chancenllor's remarks about cost sharing. This means that the students
A. disagreed with the Vice-Chancellor.
B. were surprised at the Vice-Chancellor.
C. were displeased with the Vice-Chancellor.
D. supported the Vice-Chancellor.

25. The loss of Sally's precious ring told on her. This means that Sally
A. looked in vain for her ring.
B. was very upset.
C. did not buy rings anymore.
D. kept the loss secret.

26. But for fear that his father would punish him, Jones would not have left the house. This means that
A. Jones did not leave the house.
B. Jones had wanted to stay in the house.
C. Jones' father threatened him.
D. Jones' father punished him.

27. The headmaster confused the students by talking above their heads. This means that
A. the students did not understand what was said.
B. the Headmaster shouted at the students.
C. the Headmaster complained about the students' behaviour.
D. the students did not hear what was said.

28. When we presented Mr. Owusu with the evidence, he backed down. This means that Mr. Owusu
A. refused to fulfil his promise.
B. accepted defeat.
C. denied what he had said.
D. supported our claim.

29. Kojo has been acting funny of late but has now gone round the bend. This means Kojo is
A. dead
B. mad
C. lost
D. worried

30. No sooner had we arrived than Joe left the house. This means that
A. we had not arrived soon enough to meet Joe.
B. Joe left the house well after we had arrived
C. Joe left the house as soon as we arrived.
D. Joe did not leave the house after all.
